# New Operational Source-Intake Candidates for D5 and D6

> **Repaired Markdown version.** Hidden ChatGPT citation tokens were replaced with normal Markdown links, plain organization names, and explicit source IDs. The source register at the end contains the direct URLs used in the report.

## Executive summary

The strongest new intake candidates are not generic policy PDFs. They are local and regional workflow documents that already contain executable operational artifacts: referral routes, declaration rules, staffing assumptions, role descriptions, intake triggers, multidisciplinary-overleg rules, and monitoring templates. The clearest analogue to the seed example, *Ketendocument Valpreventie Almere*, is [Ketendocument Valpreventie Lelystad](https://www.ggdflevoland.nl/app/uploads/sites/6/2025/09/Ketendocument-valpreventie-Lelystad.pdf) [S1], which includes declaration instructions, a uniform handover letter, role descriptions, a decision tree, and an Excel monitoring template. The other highest-value finds are [Proces Welzijn op Recept / Amstelland Zorg](https://amstellandzorg.nl/site/uploads/2024/03/Procesflow-Welzijn-op-Recept.pdf) [S2], [Zorgpad Vroegsignalering Zwangeren Fryslân incl. PHB-JGZ](https://www.rosfriesland.nl/wp-content/uploads/20240808-Fries-Zorgpad-Vroegsignalering-Zwangeren-Versie-2.0.pdf) [S3], [VSV protocol: kwetsbare zwangere 6.0](https://samennijmegen.nl/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/240924-protocol-kwetsbare-zwangere-6.0.pdf) [S4], [Monitoring & Evaluatie matrix Kind naar Gezonder Gewicht](https://kindnaargezondergewicht.nl/media/pages/tools/monitoring-en-evaluatie-matrix-kngg/f35eb9815f-1694178740/just_jogg_kngg_me-matrix_def.pdf) [S5], [Leidraad consultatiefunctie ggz voor het sociaal domein](https://www.menzis.nl/zorgaanbieders/-/m/publieke-sites/menzis/zorgaanbieders/downloads/zorgsoorten/ggz/consultatiefunctie/20240301-leidraad-consultatiefunctie-ggz.pdf) [S6], and [Brugfuncties tussen huisartsenzorg en sociaal domein](https://www.pharos.nl/wp-content/uploads/2025/07/Interactieve-handreiking-brugfuncties-huisartsenzorg-en-sociaal-domein_072025-SZ.pdf) [S7].  (Sources: [S1](#s1), [S2](#s2), [S3](#s3), [S4](#s4), [S5](#s5), [S6](#s6), [S7](#s7))

Across D5 and D6, the main operational gap is not the absence of thematic policy documents. It is the absence of **reusable execution artifacts**: forms, pathway sheets, handover letters, HIS/ZorgDomein routing instructions, plan-of-approach templates, staffing models, and monitoring schemas. Several secondary documents reinforce that conclusion. For example, the [Eindrapport implementatieonderzoek Prenatale Huisbezoeken JGZ](https://open.overheid.nl/documenten/f7bd4a34-ee2b-4907-ad7d-2371a7c70edf/file) [S8] explicitly notes that central and uniform PHB registration is missing, while [Meerjarenplan welzijn op recept 2023-2026](https://enschede.bestuurlijkeinformatie.nl/Document/View/399a1d8d-9eed-4889-b2b3-e8e0842d5d28) [S9] and [Transformatieplan Positieve Gezondheid | Van Klacht naar Kracht](https://gezondgooi.nl/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Transformatieplan-5-september-schone-versie.pdf) [S10] show that fixed contacts, digital referral routes, coach capacity, and social-map visibility are implementation bottlenecks rather than conceptual ones.  (Sources: [S8](#s8), [S9](#s9), [S10](#s10))

Because no repository index was available to inspect, the duplicate flags below are conservative heuristics rather than verified repository matches. I therefore treat local/regional operational PDFs as lower duplicate risk than national handreikingen and widely circulated template documents.

## Scope and selection logic

I prioritized Dutch-language primary or near-primary sources from municipal, GGD, regional-zorgakkoord, insurer, zorg-network, and field-body sites published in 2023–2026, and down-ranked generic strategy or advocacy documents. To make the cut, a document needed at least one tangible operational layer element: a workflow, route card, protocol, referral form, waiting-list or coordinator role, monitoring structure, declaration/contracting logic, or a concrete implementation/staffing table. The resulting set covers the recurring D5/D6-relevant operational clusters visible in current Dutch practice: falls prevention, Welzijn op Recept, Kansrijke Start and vulnerable-pregnancy pathways, child healthy-weight pathways, adult GLI/overweight chains, consultative ggz, mental-health network formation, bridge functions, and low-threshold support points.  (Sources: [S16](#s16), [S6](#s6), [S7](#s7), [S5](#s5))

All candidates in the main table are public PDFs. I excluded the already-mentioned [Ketendocument Valpreventie Almere](https://www.ggdflevoland.nl/app/uploads/sites/6/2026/01/Ketendocument-valpreventie-Almere.pdf) [S20] from the main list and instead focused on adjacent local variants and cross-domain analogues.

## Cross-cutting analysis

The clearest pattern is that the best documents are **document families** rather than standalone PDFs. The value sits in the annexes and child artifacts: the Lelystad falls-prevention ketendocument points to declarations, a uniform transfer letter, role descriptions, and an Excel sheet; the Amstelland WoR processflow adds HIS coding, ZorgDomein routing, intake feedback loops, and dashboard development. Intake should therefore preserve parent-child relationships instead of storing only the parent PDF.  (Sources: [S1](#s1), [S2](#s2))

Monitoring maturity differs sharply by domain. KnGG already has a concrete measurement template with fields for plan-of-approach execution, BMI, activity, collection instruments, and reporting; Lelystad falls prevention references an Excel registration sheet; WoR in Amstelland explicitly mentions dashboard development; but prenatal JGZ/PHB implementation is still hampered by weak central registration, according to the AEF implementation study. That means prenatal local pathways are especially valuable as source-intake targets, because the national layer still shows an operational data gap.  (Sources: [S5](#s5), [S1](#s1), [S2](#s2), [S8](#s8))

A second major pattern is that D6 is fundamentally about **bridging, coordination, and contractability**. The bridge-function handreiking describes the wijk-level coordinator and explicitly connects that role to multiple D5 chains; the consultative-ggz leadraad makes a regional plan the core contracting object; the MGN Rivierenland plan turns “support sources” into a governed, versioned regional work package; and the Zaanstreek-Waterland appendix translates chain ambitions into ketenprocessen, role definitions, referral agreements, and digital information transfer. These are all strong candidates because they turn cross-domain ambitions into operational specifications.  (Sources: [S7](#s7), [S6](#s6), [S14](#s14), [S16](#s16))

Several secondary documents are worth keeping on a watchlist even though they are not in the main table. [Meerjarenplan welzijn op recept 2023-2026](https://enschede.bestuurlijkeinformatie.nl/Document/View/399a1d8d-9eed-4889-b2b3-e8e0842d5d28) [S9] is useful for fixed-contact-person and social-map arrangements between houseartsen and wijkteams; [Transformatieplan Positieve Gezondheid | Van Klacht naar Kracht](https://gezondgooi.nl/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Transformatieplan-5-september-schone-versie.pdf) [S10] gives concrete WoR scale assumptions such as coach capacity and linked digital referral needs; [Handreiking Prenataal huisbezoek door de JGZ](https://www.ncj.nl/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/DEF-Handreiking-Prenataal-huisbezoek-door-de-JGZ-oktober-2025.pdf) [S18] updates consent and direct-referral practice; [Eindrapport implementatieonderzoek Prenatale Huisbezoeken JGZ](https://open.overheid.nl/documenten/f7bd4a34-ee2b-4907-ad7d-2371a7c70edf/file) [S8] explains the registration bottleneck; and [Factsheet samenwerking in een GLI-programma](https://ggdgelderlandzuid.nl/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/Factsheet-samenwerking-in-een-GLI-programma.pdf) [S19] gives a compact role map for huisarts, leefstijlcoach, buurtsportcoach, and gezondheidsmakelaar.  (Sources: [S9](#s9), [S10](#s10), [S18](#s18), [S8](#s8), [S19](#s19))

## Repository field model

The candidate set implies that the repository schema should distinguish **operational artifact type**, **process step**, and **bridge/governance logic** much more explicitly than a simple topic folder model. The same domain may produce very different intake objects: a ketendocument, a protocol, a processflow, a referral form, a monitoring matrix, a criteria set, or an insurer contracting document.  (Sources: [S1](#s1), [S2](#s2), [S5](#s5), [S6](#s6), [S16](#s16))

I would add the following repository fields:

- `artifact_type` — ketendocument, protocol, zorgpad, procesflow, referral form, monitoring matrix, criteria set, insurer policy.
- `process_step` — signalering, triage, referral, intake, MDO/casusoverleg, intervention, handover, declaration, monitoring, contracting.
- `bridge_channel` — HIS code, ZorgDomein, beveiligde mail, dashboard, social map, fixed-contact-person model.
- `actor_roles` — huisarts, POH, wijkteam, JGZ, welzijnscoach, centrale zorgverlener, ketenregisseur, ggz professional, insurer, municipality.
- `monitoring_object` — participant, plan of approach, BMI, attendance, dashboard metric, registration dataset, capacity.
- `finance_contracting_layer` — municipal funding, insurer funding, declaration rule, preferred-insurer contracting, SPUK/IZA/GALA dependency.
- `version_status` — final, concept, living document, control date, public decision copy.
- `source_family_link` — parent PDF plus annexes/templates/child assets that should be ingested together.

## Recommended next steps for intake

The first intake wave should focus on the **highest-reuse bundles**: [Ketendocument Valpreventie Lelystad](https://www.ggdflevoland.nl/app/uploads/sites/6/2025/09/Ketendocument-valpreventie-Lelystad.pdf) [S1], [Proces Welzijn op Recept / Amstelland Zorg](https://amstellandzorg.nl/site/uploads/2024/03/Procesflow-Welzijn-op-Recept.pdf) [S2], [Zorgpad Vroegsignalering Zwangeren Fryslân incl. PHB-JGZ](https://www.rosfriesland.nl/wp-content/uploads/20240808-Fries-Zorgpad-Vroegsignalering-Zwangeren-Versie-2.0.pdf) [S3], [VSV protocol: kwetsbare zwangere 6.0](https://samennijmegen.nl/wp-content/uploads/2024/11/240924-protocol-kwetsbare-zwangere-6.0.pdf) [S4], [Monitoring & Evaluatie matrix Kind naar Gezonder Gewicht](https://kindnaargezondergewicht.nl/media/pages/tools/monitoring-en-evaluatie-matrix-kngg/f35eb9815f-1694178740/just_jogg_kngg_me-matrix_def.pdf) [S5], and [Leidraad consultatiefunctie ggz voor het sociaal domein](https://www.menzis.nl/zorgaanbieders/-/m/publieke-sites/menzis/zorgaanbieders/downloads/zorgsoorten/ggz/consultatiefunctie/20240301-leidraad-consultatiefunctie-ggz.pdf) [S6]. These six already give you executable building blocks across the main D5/D6 clusters: local pathway logic, referral mechanics, monitoring structure, and contract/governance framing.  (Sources: [S1](#s1), [S2](#s2), [S3](#s3), [S4](#s4), [S5](#s5), [S6](#s6))

When ingesting the local pathway documents, the intake process should explicitly chase **linked child assets**. The Lelystad document should not be stored without its referenced declaration annex, handover letter, and Excel sheet; WoR processflows should be linked to any local referral forms, HIS codes, and dashboard definitions; and prenatal-pathway families should be linked to the PHB-JGZ consent/referral guidance and registration notes that the national PHB handreiking and AEF report identify as critical.  (Sources: [S1](#s1), [S2](#s2), [S18](#s18), [S8](#s8))

A second intake wave should target **D6 enablers** rather than only thematic D5 chains: the bridge-function handreiking, the MGN Rivierenland plan, the low-threshold-support-point criteria, the Zaanstreek-Waterland operational appendix, and the VGZ contracting document. Together these documents define how chains are coordinated, who owns the bridge role, how low-threshold support is quality-gated, how digital transfer is expected to work, and how insurer requirements affect what is operationally feasible.  (Sources: [S7](#s7), [S14](#s14), [S15](#s15), [S16](#s16), [S17](#s17))

Finally, once a repository index becomes available, the duplicate review should focus on **national reusable templates first**. The higher duplicate-risk items are the leadraden, handreikingen, and national monitoring templates; the lower duplicate-risk items are the municipality- and region-specific operational PDFs. In other words, the safest way to improve the repository quickly is to ingest local/regional execution artifacts first and use the national documents to normalize metadata, terminology, and child-document requests.  (Sources: [S1](#s1), [S2](#s2), [S4](#s4), [S5](#s5), [S6](#s6), [S7](#s7))
